What is the NIM game?

-> Nim is a mathematical game of strategy in which 2 players take turns to remove objects from distinct piles. In each turn, the player must choose one pile and remove at least 1 object from that pile. To win the game you must be the last player to remove the final set of objects!

What are the Rules?

  • In each turn, the player must remove 1 or more object from an unique pile;
  • The player cannot remove 2 or more objects from different piles in the same turn;
  • Depending on the settings defined:
    • The amount of piles can be defined by the user;
    • The user can also choose who goes first (player 1 or player 2);
    • Game can be player online (work in progress) or with a robot;
    • If SinglePlayer was selected, the player can choose the desired difficulty;
  • In case of a foul play, the game will not register the move, and will wait for a proper move;
  • Logs about the state of the game are shown below the game area (for foul plays, winning, and play information);
